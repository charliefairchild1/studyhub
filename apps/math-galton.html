Why PEP (and almost every ML loss) loves Gaussians. Residuals are rarely "a single cause" — they're piles of small independent effects. CLT says those piles go Gaussian. MSE loss is the max-likelihood estimator under Gaussian residuals; gradient descent under MSE is Bayesian update under a Gaussian likelihood. Primitive 3 defaults to Gaussian not by choice but by theorem.

Connected demos. ◌ Diffusion — the Gaussian kernel is exactly the limit of many random-walk steps. Σ Entropy — Gaussian is max-entropy for a fixed variance. Β Bayes — Normal prior × Normal likelihood = Normal posterior (conjugacy).
