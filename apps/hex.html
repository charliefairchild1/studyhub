Notes

What you're seeing: at each turn, the algorithm computes max-flow for both players — how many independent paths each could still build. The surplus is the difference. Player 1 plays the move that maximizes it; Player 2 plays the move that minimizes it.

The invariant: if the surplus stays positive after every Player 1 move, the chain inevitably completes and Player 1 wins. This is the constructive form of Nash's strategy-stealing argument.

The chicken connection: exactly the same math — count available paths, count opponent's blocks, commit when surplus is positive — used in real biological hardware to cross roads.
