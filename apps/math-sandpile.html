Sandpile

Drop one grain. If a cell reaches 4, it gives 1 grain to each of its 4 neighbors and resets. Cascades happen. The avalanche-size distribution becomes a power law without any tuning — the system self-organizes to criticality.

What "self-organized criticality" means. Most phase-transition systems need you to dial a parameter to its critical value (T_c in Ising, p_c in percolation). Sandpile doesn't — it lives at criticality automatically because of the slow input + threshold release dynamic. Brains, forest fires, earthquakes, and stock markets all show this pattern.

The slope τ ≈ 1.0–1.2 in 2D. If you see something else, the system hasn't equilibrated yet — let it run longer.
