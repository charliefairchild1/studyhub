Percolation

Each cell is filled with probability p. Filled neighbors share a cluster. At p_c ≈ 0.5927 (site percolation on square lattice) a giant component appears that spans the whole grid.

Why PEP cares. Spreading activation on a random graph has the same transition at a critical edge density. Below: seed dies locally — retrieval fails for most queries. Above: one seed reaches everything — retrieval is dominated by popularity, not relevance. Retrieval systems tune density to sit just above p_c: connected but not saturated.

Also appears in: forest fires, disease spread (see ☣ Epidemic), social tipping, neural-avalanche criticality (brains seem to operate near p_c — "edge of chaos" hypothesis).

Try this

p = 0.5: many small clusters. No spanning path.
p = 0.59–0.60: fractal "incipient infinite cluster" — spanning for some seeds, not others.
p = 0.65: one giant cluster, isolated holes. Phase has flipped.
Build ensemble curve: runs 20 lattices per p, averages span probability. The S-curve around p_c is the phase transition.
