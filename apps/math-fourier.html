PEP primitive 3. The k-term reconstruction IS a predictor. Each new term reduces residual energy by exactly |ak|². The residual-shrink rate is a property of the signal, not the predictor — it's set by how concentrated the signal's spectrum is.

Tie to entropy. A signal whose power is spread uniformly across all frequencies (white noise) has maximum spectral entropy and minimum predictability — no finite k helps. A signal concentrated on a few frequencies (voice, music) is low-entropy and compresses fast. Predictor ceiling = spectral entropy of the residual.
