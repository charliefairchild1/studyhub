Where the number comes from

▲ Transformation (the case)

  • Reusability = anti-dissipation, literally. Landing & re-flying boosters cut $/kg ~10×+ — more output per unit of burned capital. That's the PTO edge as an engineering fact.
  • Launch near-monopoly. ~80–90% of all mass to orbit; cadence competitors can't match yet.
  • Starlink converts capex into recurring revenue — a compounding cash engine with a global TAM (rural, maritime, aviation, mobile, defense).
  • Starship is optionality, not priced-in cash: heavy lift, Mars, point-to-point — a call option on a step-change.
  • Vertical integration + iteration speed — hardware-rich testing turns potential into learning faster than anyone.

▼ Dissipation (the risks)

  • Key-person concentration — Musk is the org's spine and its political/attention risk.
  • Capital intensity — Starship + Starlink v3 burn enormous cash; execution can slip.
  • Competition — Amazon Kuiper, Blue Origin, China's launch + constellations.
  • Regulatory / spectrum / geopolitical friction (FAA, ITU, foreign markets).
  • Illiquidity — private marks are sticky and can reprice hard; you may not be able to exit.

If you actually wanted exposure (all with real caveats)

  • DXYZ (Destiny Tech100) — a public closed-end fund that holds SpaceX. Buyable in Robinhood, but it has traded at a wild premium to its actual net asset value — you can overpay massively for the SpaceX slice. Big risk.
