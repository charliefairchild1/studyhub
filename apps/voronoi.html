What this shows

Every time PEP retrieves a concept, it computes nearest-neighbor in embedding space. The set of all query points that would retrieve concept X forms X's Voronoi cell. The full tessellation IS the retrieval partition — PEP's implicit map of "what's nearest to what."

Click anywhere to place a concept seed (labeled automatically). Move the golden query point (drag it) to see which concept wins the retrieval at that position, and the distances to its 3 nearest neighbors.

PEP primitives used

Weighted graph: each seed is a node.
Spreading activation: the gradient of distance from query is the activation decay.
Predictor + residual: "nearest" is a prediction — moving the query around tests where the predictor is confident vs. where cells are narrow and it could be wrong.

The Voronoi diagram is the ground truth of what PEP retrieves; every retrieval system is an approximation of this exact geometry.
