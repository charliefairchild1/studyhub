What day of the week?

The trick: every year has one weekday — its Doomsday — that a handful of easy dates always land on. Learn those dates, find the year's Doomsday, then count from the nearest one.

1 · The memorable Doomsday dates

These all fall on the year's Doomsday:

Even months4/4, 6/6, 8/8, 10/10, 12/12
Odd months5/9, 9/5, 7/11, 11/7 — "9-to-5 at 7-Eleven"
Marchthe last day of February (2/28, or 2/29 in a leap year), and March 7
January1/3 (common year) or 1/4 (leap year)

2 · Find the year's Doomsday

Anchor per century (the Doomsday of the "00" year):

1800sFriday
1900sWednesday
2000sTuesday
2100sSunday

For year YY in the 2000s: take YY + (YY ÷ 4, drop remainder), add the anchor (Tuesday = 2), then mod 7. That weekday is this year's Doomsday.

Example — 2025: 25 + 6 = 31; +2 = 33; 33 mod 7 = 5 → Friday. So 4/4, 6/6, 12/12 of 2025 are all Fridays.

3 · Count to your date

Find the memorable Doomsday nearest your date, then step by weeks and days. Oct 10 is a Doomsday, so Oct 13 is Doomsday + 3.
