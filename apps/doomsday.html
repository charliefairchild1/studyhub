How To Do It

Every year has a "doomsday" which is a day of the week. Some dates in each month always land on that day. If you know those dates you can count to the day you want.

Steps:

  1. Get the century day. 1900s = Wednesday, 2000s = Tuesday, 1800s = Friday, 1700s = Sunday.
  2. Take the last 2 digits of the year (call it y). Do (y + (y divided by 4, no remainder) + century day) and take the remainder when you divide by 7.
  3. That number is the doomsday. 0=Sunday, 1=Monday, ... 6=Saturday.
  4. Find the doomsday date for the month (see chart). Count days from there to your date.

The doomsday dates to memorize:

  • Jan 3 (or Jan 4 on leap years)
  • Feb 28 (or 29 on leap years)
  • March 14
  • 4/4, 6/6, 8/8, 10/10, 12/12
  • 5/9, 9/5, 7/11, 11/7 (think "9 to 5 at the 7 eleven")
