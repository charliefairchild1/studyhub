Lorenz Attractor

Two trajectories start ε apart in state space. Both live on the butterfly. But their positions diverge exponentially — that's the prediction horizon. After O(log(1/ε)) time, any predictor loses lock on the exact state, no matter how perfect its model.

Why this matters to PEP. Primitive 3 (predictor + residual) has a finite horizon in any chaotic system. Residual stays bounded at long lead times no matter how much you train — the signal itself has outgrown what a predictor can resolve. Lesson: predict the attractor (shape, statistics, regime) instead of the state. PTO echoes this: grade the trajectory, not the instantaneous position.
